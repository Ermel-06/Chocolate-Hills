About the Chocolate Hills

The Chocolate Hills are a geological formation located in the Bohol province of the Philippines. Comprising over 1,200 perfectly cone-shaped hills, they turn a rich brown color during the dry season, resembling chocolate mounds, hence the name.

These hills are made of limestone covered with grass, and their unique shape is the result of centuries of erosion. The Chocolate Hills are a popular tourist destination, offering stunning panoramic views from observation decks and opportunities for hiking and photography.

Recognized as a National Geological Monument, the Chocolate Hills are not only a natural wonder but also a symbol of the Philippines' rich geological heritage.

Attractions & Activities

From panoramic viewpoints to adventurous hikes, explore the various attractions and activities that make Chocolate Hills a memorable destination for all visitors.

Chocolate Hills Viewpoint

Enjoy a stunning view of hundreds of hills from a high viewing deck. This spot is perfect for pictures and seeing the beauty of the Chocolate Hills up close.

Sagbayan Peak

A family-friendly viewpoint with gardens, playgrounds, and viewing decks. You can also spot tarsiers here and take relaxing photos with beautiful landscapes.

Chocolate Hills Adventure Park

Experience adventure activities such as:

  • Bike Zipline ("The Rush")
  • Rope Courses
  • Tree Challenges

This is a great place for thrill-seekers and nature lovers.

Dry Season

The dry season brings sunny skies and the iconic brown “chocolate” color of the hills, making it the best time for sightseeing and photos. It can get hot during midday, so stay hydrated and wear sunscreen.

Wet Season (Rainy Season)

The hills turn lush green during the rainy season, offering cool weather and peaceful, misty views. Expect occasional rain showers, so bring an umbrella or raincoat and waterproof bags for your things.
