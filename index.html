About the Chocolate Hills

The Chocolate Hills are a geological formation located in the Bohol province of the Philippines. Comprising over 1,200 perfectly cone-shaped hills, they turn a rich brown color during the dry season, resembling chocolate mounds, hence the name.

Formed over thousands of years by natural forces, these hills continue to amaze geologists and travelers alike. Legend tells of giants whose tears shaped the hills, adding a touch of magic to their beauty.
